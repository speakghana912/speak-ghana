Our Story

SPIL Ghana was born from two journeys — one in Russia, one in Ghana.

Richard grew up in Ghana but moved abroad for studies. When he arrived in Russia speaking zero Russian, he discovered that language isn't taught — it's shared. He learned by listening, speaking badly at first, and never giving up.

Meanwhile, Charlotte was in Ghana, studying Akan at university, earning her teaching qualifications, and learning what it takes to help someone truly understand a language. She saw how many diaspora Ghanaians longed to speak their mother tongue but didn't know where to start.

Together, they decided to build something. A place where the diaspora could come to learn Ghanaian languages — not from apps or textbooks, but from trained educators who understand both the language and the journey of reconnecting.

That's SPIL Ghana. Speak Indigenous Languages of Ghana.

What We Teach

We specialize in Ghana's indigenous languages:

  • Twi (Akan) — The most widely spoken Ghanaian language
  • Ga — The language of Accra and its people
  • Ewe — Spoken in the Volta Region and beyond
  • Fante — The coastal language of Central and Western Regions
  • Dagbani — The language of Northern Ghana

We offer live online classes for adults, kids, and teens — designed specifically for diaspora learners who want to speak, not just study.

Our Approach

💬

Speak 80% of Class Time

You learn to talk by talking. We make sure you're speaking most of the class.

🎮

Games & Stories

Language lives in joy. We use games, stories, and songs — not boring drills.

⏱️

Patience Over Perfection

Mistakes are how you learn. We never rush you. We never shame you. You'll get there.

Our Promise to You

Whether you only know "Akwaaba" or you can already hold a simple conversation — come as you are. We'll meet you where you are and walk with you from there.

Language is not just words. It's connection. It's home. Let us help you find yours.
