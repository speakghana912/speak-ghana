From Syllables to Sentences: Adelaide's First Month with Speak Ghana

Adelaide Daniels (Kakra)

Speak Ghana Student – March 2026 Cohort | UK-based diaspora learner

"I have been taking lessons for a month now, and I am now able to form simple sentences from the words I have been taught."

The teachers are very attentive and help whenever I need it. I love that there are multiple teachers — one helps more with speaking, and when you need something written down or have simple questions, another teacher is available in the chat.

I feel confident reading the alphabet and I love how vocabulary is so naturally built throughout the lessons. The teachers are also funny and very kind, so it makes the lessons more enjoyable and fun!

I love the pacing of the class — it's slow enough that you can go over things to ensure understanding, but fast enough that you can see the progress every week with reading, speaking and writing!

Syllables Adelaide Has Mastered

GY — Gye (take), Gyina (stand/stop)
HY — Hyia (meet), Ahyɛn (vehicle)
HW — Hwan (who), Hwehwɛ (looking/searching)
KW — Kwaku (Wednesday-born male), Akwantuo (journey)
KY — Ɔkyerɛkyerɛni (teacher), Akyire (later)
NY — Nyɛ (do not), Nya (to get)
NW — Nwoma (book), Nwunu (cold)
TW — Twa (cut), Twitwa (chop e.g food)

Real Sentences Adelaide Can Now Say

  1. Nwoma a m'ani gye ho pa ara ne Matilda — My favourite book is Matilda
  2. Merekɔtwa amango dua no — I'm going to chop the mango tree
  3. Hwɛ! Me Maame ho yɛ fɛ! — Look! My mum is beautiful!
  4. Kyerɛ me kwan no — Show me the way
  5. Mehyiaa me nua no anɔpa yi — I met my sibling this morning
